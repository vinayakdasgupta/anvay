About anvay

A tool for exploring thematic structure in Bengali texts

The word anvay (অন্বয়) has layered meanings in Bengali: it refers to connection, syntax, succession, logical consequence, and even lineage. This project takes its name from this rich semantic field, evoking the idea of tracing meaningful relationships through language.

anvay is an open-source application for topic modeling and visual interpretation of Bengali-language corpora. It is designed to support digital humanities research, literary analysis, and linguistic inquiry. Built around Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA), Anvay integrates custom Bengali preprocessing workflows, structured visualizations, and relevance scoring to enhance the interpretability of computational topics.

Unlike general-purpose topic modeling tools, Anvay has been tailored for the specific challenges of working with morphologically rich and low-resource languages. It enables scholars, students, and researchers to upload their own textual materials and receive a structured breakdown of latent themes, token frequency distributions, and interpretive clues.

The software is released under the MIT License. Documentation and UI text are available under a Creative Commons Attribution 4.0 License.

Anvay was designed and developed with support from OpenAI’s ChatGPT.

If you use Anvay in a scholarly publication, please consider citing it or linking to the project repository.
