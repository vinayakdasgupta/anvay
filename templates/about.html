About anvay

A tool for exploring thematic structure in text

The word anvay (অন্বয়) carries layered meanings in Bengali: connection, syntax, succession, logical consequence, lineage. This project takes its name from that rich semantic field — the idea of tracing meaningful relationships through language.

anvay is an open-source application for topic modelling and visual interpretation of text corpora. It is designed to support digital humanities research, literary analysis, and linguistic inquiry across multiple languages. Built around Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA), anvay integrates language-specific preprocessing workflows, structured visualisations, and relevance scoring to enhance the interpretability of computational topics.

Unlike general-purpose topic modelling tools, anvay has been built with the specific challenges of South Asian languages in mind — morphological richness, script complexity, and the relative scarcity of NLP resources compared to English. It enables scholars, students, and researchers to upload their own textual materials and receive a structured breakdown of latent themes, token frequency distributions, and interpretive clues.

Language Support

anvay currently supports four languages. Bengali is the primary supported language, with the most mature and extensively tested preprocessing pipeline. English, Hindi, and Tamil are experimental — functional, but less validated, and we welcome feedback from researchers working in these languages.

Language Script Tokeniser Normalisation Status
Bengali Bengali script Custom (zero-width aware) Dictionary-based stemming Primary
English Latin NLTK word_tokenize WordNet lemmatisation Experimental
Hindi Devanagari Custom Devanagari simplemma lemmatisation Experimental
Tamil Tamil script Custom Tamil Dictionary-based stemming Experimental
Lexical Resources

The Bengali preprocessing pipeline includes a dictionary-based stemmer derived from lemma mappings made available by the BanglaNLP project. We gratefully acknowledge the makers and curators of that resource. The Tamil stemmer similarly draws on a curated dictionary. Full citations are included in the source code.
